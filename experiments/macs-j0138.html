MACS J0138 — NFW Fit & θE Cross-Check

Cluster lens: estimate NFW parameters and validate against published constraints.
HST / MAST Strong Lensing NFW Profile

Objective

Estimate NFW parameters $$\\rho_s,\\ r_s$$ for MACS J0138 and check internal consistency with the Einstein radius $$\\theta_E$$ and/or the convergence profile $$\\kappa(R)$$.

Method (High-Level)

  1. Adopt cosmology and fix $$z_L, z_S$$.
  2. Fit $$\\rho_s, r_s$$ using $$\\theta_E$$ and/or $$\\kappa(R)$$ with uncertainties.
  3. Cross-check with published values; log parameter deltas.
  4. Save artifacts: nfw_fit.json, plots in figures/, sources in data/.

Einstein-radius condition

$$\\bar{\\kappa}(\\theta_E)=1$$

What does this mean?

  • $$\\theta_E$$ is like the “ring size” of the lens; matching it checks the mass scale.
  • $$\\rho_s, r_s$$ are the “density” and “scale radius” knobs of the dark-matter halo.
  • Good matches → lens consistent with a normal NFW halo. Mismatches → diagnostics for refining assumptions or falsifying theory.
